Where the name comes from

Jatin Chopra

I like knowing where my own name comes from, so here's my short version of it.

Jatin
first name

One of the names for Shiva — the god of destruction, but also, in the stories I grew up with, the god who is easiest to please. I've always liked that combination: something that can tear down and rebuild, and doesn't need much to be content.

Chopra
family name

A surname tied to the Khatri community of Punjab — historically associated with trade and soldiering, people who showed up for a fight when it mattered. I like to think some of that shows up in how I approach a stubborn dataset.

Academic path

From India to Germany

I finished my BSc in Physics (with a minor in Mathematics and Electronics) at Savitribai Phule Pune University with a CGPA of 9.77, then moved to Bonn in 2023 for my MSc in Astrophysics at AIfA, expected to graduate in November 2026.

2023 – 2026 · Bonn, Germany

MSc Astrophysics, Universität Bonn

Thesis on spatially resolved quenching in post-merger galaxies, supervised by Dr. Mallory Thorp. Along the way: resolved BPT AGN classification in MaNGA, VLBA maser scattering with MPIfR, photometric bulge decomposition, and Bayesian cloud-chemistry modelling with Universiteit Leiden. Alongside the research, I've been tutoring AIfA's optical astronomy lab and getting regular telescope time in for astrophotography on the rooftop.

2019 – 2022 · Pune, India

BSc Physics, Savitribai Phule Pune University

Minor in Mathematics and Electronics. Ran group discussions with fellow students on topics spanning astrophysics, machine learning, condensed matter physics, and nuclear physics — basically whatever corner of STEM someone wanted to argue about that week. Secured the department's highest grades.

Outside the terminal

What I do when I'm not staring at galaxies

I write poems when a thought won't leave me alone, usually with indie folk or a good ghazal playing in the background — heartbreak, it turns out, translates well across genres and languages. I'm also, somewhat inexplicably, a bit of a railway enthusiast, happy to discuss gauges and timetables longer than is strictly socially advisable. Most evenings I'm either at the gym or out on a run, currently training for an IRONMAN, and whenever I can get my hands on a motorbike I go looking for a mountain pass to take it over — Mig La and Umling La in Ladakh are two I've already ticked off. The list gets more ambitious from there: Annapurna (26,545 ft) and Everest (29,032 ft) are both on it. AI nerd, gym freak, runner mind — pick whichever fits the moment.

Outreach & teaching

Passing it on

AIfA · Aug 2025 – Nov 2026

Tutor, Optical Astronomy Exoplanet Transit Lab

I explain the observation, calibration, and data-reduction pipeline of a modern optical telescope to MSc students, conduct oral examinations, carry out telescope observations, supervise data reduction, and grade lab reports. I also give tours of the telescope on the AIfA rooftop to visiting students and guests.

Naxxatra Sciences · Jan – Feb 2023, Bangalore

Research and Teaching Fellow

I ran hands-on astrophysics lectures for 300 students across grades 5–12, covering galaxy morphology, the cosmic distance ladder, how different telescopes work, exoplanet-detection methods, and an introduction to dark matter and the CMB. I also led experiments like sunspot tracking, measuring the acceleration due to gravity, and calculating Earth's circumference, and supervised solar observations, stargazing sessions, and a comet C/2022 E3 (ZTF) observation night for students, faculty, and parents.
